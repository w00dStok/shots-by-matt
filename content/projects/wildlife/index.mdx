Wildlife lives beside us more often than we realize. A robin on a fence post, a deer slipping through the edge of a field, a crow watching from a streetlight. These quiet neighbors move through the same spaces we do, following rhythms far older than our roads and houses. To notice them, even for a moment, is a gentle reminder that the world is shared. We are not alone in these places. We are simply one part of the living landscape.
